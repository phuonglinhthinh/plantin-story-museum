Discover the legacy of a man who turned printing into an art and knowledge into power.

Who was Christopher Plantin

Christophe Plantin (1520–1589) was a visionary printer who built one of the greatest printing houses of the Renaissance.
From humble beginnings as a bookbinder, he built the Plantin Press into a world-renowned printing house, celebrated for its precision and quality.
His work made knowledge more accessible and brought precision to the art of printing.

But Plantin’s story wasn’t always rooted in France.

A Journey of Opportunity

Plantin moved to Antwerp, a bustling hub of trade, culture, and ideas. In 1555, he opened the doors to his workshop, the Officina Plantiniana, and started printing books that would travel the world.

Plantin’s workshop was enormous for its time. At its peak, it had 22 printing presses and 80 workers, each contributing to the meticulous art of book production.
